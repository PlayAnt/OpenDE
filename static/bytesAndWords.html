Bytes and Words

Bytes

A byte is a unit that is a series of 8 bits, its unit symbol is a capital B. A byte, can represent the numbers 0-255 and therefore has 256 different combinations. A kB is a kilobyte and contains 1000 kB which is simple to calculate with, but that is based on powers of 10. Bits and bytes are not based on powers of 10 like kilograms and kilometer, therefore another unit has been made for large amounts of bytes. In the table below we illustrate how larger amounts of bytes are described the decimal and the official IEC way.

Metric Value IEC Value
kilobyte (kB) 1000 kibibyte (kiB) 1024
megabyte (MB) 1000² mebibyte (MiB) 1024²
giabyte (GB) 1000³ gigibyte (GiB) 1024³
terabyte (TB) 1000⁴ tebibyte (TiB) 1024⁴

Words

A byte can, as previously said, only has 256 combinations. In most computers you want to be able to compute with larger numbers, and often you also want to be able to store large amount of data. Storing large amount of data means you require a larger amount of adressess. That is where words come in, they are the unit of data used by a particular processor design. The word length of modern computers is most often 16, 32 or 64 bits. Since each additional bit doubles the range of a word 16 bits can represent a much larger number, consider the table bellow that shows word length and its nubmer of combinations available.

Word Length Combinations
8 256
16 65,536
32 4,294,967,296
64 18,446,744,073,709,551,616
